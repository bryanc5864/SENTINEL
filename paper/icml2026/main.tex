\begin{abstract}
Water contamination is detected today by single-modality systems---in-situ sensors, satellite remote sensing, microbial assays, toxicogenomic screens, and behavioral biomonitors---that operate independently and cannot compensate for each other's blind spots. With 2.2 billion people lacking reliably safe drinking water, a unified multimodal approach is urgently needed. We present \textbf{SENTINEL}, a deep-learning stack that fuses all five modalities into a single waterway-state representation. SENTINEL pairs modality-specific encoders---continuous-time SSMs for sensor streams, masked-autoencoder ViTs for Sentinel-2 imagery, Aitchison-geometry transformers for compositional microbial data, biology-constrained P-Net hierarchies for gene-expression toxicology, and diffusion-pretrained trajectory encoders for organism behavior---with a Perceiver~IO cross-attention fusion layer featuring learned per-modality temporal decay and confidence gating, and a PPO-trained cascade controller that activates expensive modalities only when warranted. To train and evaluate SENTINEL we release \textbf{SENTINEL-DB}, a harmonised database of $390$\,M+ records from 13 public sources covering 105 countries and 94k sites. On a 31-event historical contamination benchmark spanning algal blooms, industrial spills, and hypoxic dead zones, full fusion attains AUROC $0.992$ (95\% CI $[0.991, 0.993]$) versus $0.943$ for the best single modality ($p\!=\!1.6\!\times\!10^{-3}$). The system detects every event ahead of its public advisory date with a mean lead time of 32~days while producing zero false positives across 8,739 windows on ten clean reference sites. Decision tree distillation of the cascade controller enables GPU-free field deployment. We release code, model weights, and SENTINEL-DB as a community benchmark for multimodal environmental monitoring.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Each available sensing modality for water quality has well-known failure modes: in-situ probes drift and miss compounds outside their calibration; satellites cannot see through clouds or at night; microbial profiles take days; toxicogenomic and behavioral assays are sensitive but expensive. Existing systems pick \emph{one} modality and accept its blind spots.

Multimodal fusion can exploit complementary information---our information audit places the sensor--molecular pair at $I\!=\!0.0$ nats mutual information, with population complementarity $0.042$---degrade gracefully when modalities are unavailable, and enable cross-modal transfer. Three practical obstacles motivate SENTINEL: heterogeneous sampling rates, the absence of a unified benchmark, and the cost of activating all modalities at every site.

\textbf{Contributions.}
(i)~A five-encoder stack matched to each modality's geometry;
(ii)~asynchronous Perceiver~IO fusion with temporal decay and a PPO-trained cascade controller distillable into a depth-6 decision tree;
(iii)~SENTINEL-DB, a harmonised $390$M+ record database from 13 sources;
(iv)~a 20-experiment evaluation including a 31-event historical benchmark with all results released as JSON/CSV.

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

ML-based water-quality monitoring treats single modalities in isolation: sensor anomaly detection~\citep{olden2008ml_ecology, guidotti2024watermlsurvey}, Sentinel-2 regression~\citep{drusch2012sentinel2}, microbial source tracking~\citep{thompson2017earthmicrobiome}, and gene-expression screening. Cross-modal benchmarks that span these domains are absent, and what fusion has been attempted is typically limited to two modalities trained on small bespoke datasets. Our architecture draws on Perceiver~IO~\citep{jaegle2022perceiverio} for modality-agnostic fusion, SSMs~\citep{gu2022s4, gu2024mamba} for irregular sequences, MAE/ViT~\citep{he2022mae, dosovitskiy2021vit} for spatial backbones, P-Net~\citep{elmarakeby2021pnet} for biology-constrained hierarchies, and Aitchison geometry~\citep{aitchison1982} for compositional data. We cast cost-aware modality activation as a finite-horizon MDP solved with PPO~\citep{schulman2017ppo}.

\section{The SENTINEL Stack}
\label{sec:method}

SENTINEL comprises five modality-specific encoders that project raw observations into a shared $256$-d embedding space, a Perceiver~IO fusion layer that maintains a recurrent latent waterway state, and a cascade controller that selects which modalities to query at runtime. Encoders are self-supervised pretrained on each modality's largest unlabelled corpus, then fine-tuned on a contamination-detection objective. Full training schedules are in Appendix~\ref{app:hp}.

\textbf{AquaSSM} (sensor) handles six water-quality parameters at $15$-min cadence. We adopt a continuous-time S4~\citep{gu2022s4} model with eight parallel channels spanning $1$\,h to $1$\,yr (Figure~\ref{fig:aquassm}), and a physics-constraint loss penalising temperature--DO pairs that violate Henry's law. Pretrained on $20{,}000$ USGS-NWIS sequences from $1{,}115$ stations; AUROC~$0.916$ ($+0.052$ vs.\ MCN-LSTM).

\textbf{HydroViT} (satellite) is a ViT-S/16 with MAE pretraining on $2{,}986$ Sentinel-2 L2A tiles (10 bands, 10/20\,m) and multi-resolution cross-attention fusing the $10$\,m and $20$\,m bands (Figure~\ref{fig:hydrovit}). Water temperature $R^2\!=\!0.893$ ($+0.009$ vs.\ DenseNet121); a spectral-physics auxiliary loss enforces known band-ratio relationships.

\textbf{ToxiGene} (molecular) is a $145.2$M-param biology-constrained P-Net~\citep{elmarakeby2021pnet} hierarchy: gene$\rightarrow$pathway$\rightarrow$process$\rightarrow$outcome with Reactome sparsity masks and an autoencoder bottleneck (Figure~\ref{fig:toxigene}). Cross-species ortholog alignment enables transfer between zebrafish, \emph{Daphnia magna}, and fathead minnow. First-in-class 7-class toxicant classification from RNA-seq; macro-F$_1\!=\!0.877$.

\textbf{BioMotion} (behavioral) uses diffusion pretraining~\citep{ho2020ddpm} on healthy \emph{Daphnia} trajectories with per-species keypoint configurations (Figure~\ref{fig:biomotion}). At inference, denoising reconstruction error flags toxicant exposure. AUROC~$1.000$ on $17{,}074$ EPA ECOTOX assays ($+0.042$ vs.\ Deep Autoencoder).

\subsection{Asynchronous Fusion and Cascade Control}
\label{sec:fusion}

Modality embeddings arrive at vastly different rates---sensor in minutes, behavioral in hours, satellite in days, microbial in weeks. We project each embedding into the shared $256$-d space and register it with its arrival time. The fusion layer maintains a learned latent array $L\!\in\!\mathbb{R}^{256\times 256}$; each embedding is weighted by exponential decay $\exp(-\lambda_m(t\!-\!t_m))$ with learned half-lives (sensor $2$\,h, satellite $5$\,d, microbial $7$\,d), passed through a per-modality confidence gate $g_m\!\in\![0,1]$, and read into $L$ via 8-head cross-attention. Four self-attention layers then refine $L$. Heads decode to an anomaly score, 8-class source attribution, and per-modality attention weights.

Activating all five modalities at every site is operationally infeasible. We cast modality activation as a finite-horizon MDP with reward $r_t\!=\!\Delta\mathrm{AUROC}_t - \beta\,c_t$ and per-modality costs (sensor $5$, satellite $0.5$, microbial $15$, molecular $25$, behavioral $10$). We train with PPO~\citep{schulman2017ppo} for $500$\,k timesteps and distil into a depth-6 decision tree on $50$\,k state--action pairs for GPU-free field deployment.

\section{SENTINEL-DB}
\label{sec:db}

We release a harmonised database of $390$\,M+ records from 13 sources covering 105 countries and 94k sites (Table~\ref{tab:db}). A \emph{unified parameter ontology} resolves $>$10k raw names into $\sim$500 canonical parameters. \emph{H3 hexagonal indexing} at resolution 8 enables cross-source spatial joins. \emph{Quality tiers} (Q1--Q4) propagate through training losses and inference gates. A \emph{type-safe Pydantic v2 schema} captures each record with full provenance.

\textbf{Fusion and ablation.}
Full 5-modality fusion attains \textbf{AUROC $0.9919$} (95\% bootstrap CI $[0.9907, 0.9934]$), against $0.9429$ for sensor-only ($p\!=\!1.6\!\times\!10^{-3}$, paired permutation, $n_\text{perm}\!=\!10{,}000$). Mean AUROC rises with cardinality: $|\mathcal{M}|\!=\!1\!:\!0.725$, $2\!:\!0.849$, $3\!:\!0.913$, $4\!:\!0.952$, $5\!:\!0.992$. Sensor and behavioral carry most of the discriminative signal; microbial and molecular are largely redundant for detection but crucial for source attribution. Full ablation details and additional subset combinations are in Appendix~\ref{app:ablation}.

\textbf{Historical benchmark.}
The benchmark spans 31 contamination events: algal blooms on Lake Erie, Chesapeake Bay, Klamath River; industrial spills on the Animas River and East Palestine; and hypoxia-driven events including the Gulf of Mexico dead zone. SENTINEL detects \emph{every} event ahead of its public advisory date with a mean lead time of $32$~days (Figure~\ref{fig:earlywarning}), ranging from $3.3$\,d (Toledo) to $87$\,d (Chesapeake hypoxia 2018). Events with nutrient-loading causal pathways are flagged tens of days early; sudden chemical spills are caught only days early because their causal lag is short.

\textbf{Operational reliability.}
On ten clean NEON reference sites, the alert rate at threshold $0.9$ is $0\%$ across $8{,}739$ windows, yielding a $31\!:\!0$ persistence ratio and a $12.8\times$ signal-to-noise gap. MC-dropout ($50$ passes) ECE drops to $0.034$ after temperature scaling. Conformal prediction~\citep{angelopoulos2021conformal, vovk2005conformal} achieves $96.3\%$ satellite coverage and $93.4\%$ overall at $\alpha\!=\!0.05$ (Appendix~\ref{app:conformal}). Causal-chain mining (PCMCI+) over $20$ GRQA sites discovers $375$ chains across $91$ types with $44$ novel (Appendix~\ref{app:causal}).

\section{Limitations and Conclusion}

The behavioral channel's near-perfect AUROC reflects controlled EPA ECOTOX labels, not field deployment; we down-weight behavioral evidence in the cascade controller. Several benchmark events have advisory dates rather than continuous ground-truth labels. ToxiGene transfer is bounded by ortholog coverage. SENTINEL-DB inherits sampling biases with North American and European records dominant.

A modality-respecting encoder stack, asynchronous Perceiver~IO fusion, and a cost-aware cascade controller yield zero false positives across $8{,}739$ clean-site windows, $31/31$ events flagged ahead of advisories, and a fusion AUROC that significantly improves over the best single modality. We release the model stack, training scripts, and SENTINEL-DB as a community benchmark for multimodal environmental monitoring.

\section{Hyperparameters and Training Details}
\label{app:hp}

Full training schedules, learning-rate ramps, batch sizes, and config files are in \texttt{configs/default.yaml} of the released code. Each encoder is pretrained for $20$ epochs with AdamW ($\eta\!=\!3{\times}10^{-4}$, weight decay $0.05$), then fine-tuned end-to-end. Fusion is trained for $50$ epochs with frozen encoder backbones, then $10$ epochs of joint fine-tuning. PPO uses $\gamma\!=\!0.99$, $\lambda_\text{GAE}\!=\!0.95$, clip $0.2$, entropy bonus $0.01$, rollout length $2{,}048$.

\section{Baseline Comparison Protocols}
\label{app:baselines}

Two protocols appear in the paper. \emph{Windowed event detection}: each $24$-h window in a $\pm 90$-day envelope around an advisory date is scored, and AUROC is computed across windows pooled over the $31$ events. \emph{Point detection on a stratified NEON anomaly subset}: $1{,}000$ windows ($500$ normal, $500$ threshold-exceeding) from a single NEON site. Classical baselines (Z-score $0.59$, isolation forest $0.65$, ARIMA $0.61$) are competitive on point detection but decisively worse on windowed events.

\section{Cascade Controller Details}
\label{app:cascade}

PPO state $s\!\in\!\mathbb{R}^{267}$ (\S\ref{sec:fusion}). Reward $r_t\!=\!\Delta\mathrm{AUROC}_t-\beta c_t$ with per-step modality cost and $\beta$ tuned on held-out sites. The trained policy is distilled into a depth-$6$ decision tree via behavioral cloning on $50$\,k state--action pairs.

\section{Broader Impact}
\label{app:impact}

SENTINEL is intended for public-health and environmental protection. The same model that detects contamination can in principle help an adversary evade detection; we mitigate by cascading multiple orthogonal modalities. An AI alarm should not replace standard chain-of-custody sampling for regulatory enforcement. Predictive sensor placement could reinforce monitoring inequities; we couple the placement objective with population-vulnerability constraints.
