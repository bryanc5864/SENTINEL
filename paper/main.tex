\title{SENTINEL: A Multimodal AI Framework for Early Water Pollution Detection through Cross-Modal Temporal Fusion}
\author{Bryan Cheng}
\date{\today}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
We present SENTINEL, the first multimodal AI framework that unifies five
environmental sensing modalities---physicochemical sensor time series, satellite
remote sensing, aquatic metagenomics, transcriptomic stress biomarkers, and
organismal behavioral signals---for early water pollution detection. SENTINEL
introduces five novel modality-specific architectures: AquaSSM (continuous-time
state space model for irregular sensor data), HydroViT (water-specific vision
foundation model for satellite imagery), MicroBiomeNet (Aitchison-geometry-aware
deep learning for compositional microbiome data), ToxiGene (pathway-informed
transcriptomic classifier with minimal biomarker panel discovery), and BioMotion
(diffusion-pretrained behavioral anomaly detector). These encoders feed into
SENTINEL-Fusion, a Perceiver IO-derived cross-modal temporal attention framework
that handles asynchronous, heterogeneous data streams with missing modalities as
the norm. We contribute SENTINEL-DB, a large-scale multimodal water quality dataset
(${\sim}85$\,GB; 390M+ records from thirteen public sources), and three theoretical contributions
including cross-modal transfer bounds and distribution-free conformal anomaly
detection. Trained on real data from NEON, USGS, EPA, GRQA, Sentinel-2, Earth Microbiome
Project, and NCBI GEO, SENTINEL achieves AUROC = 0.920 for sensor anomaly
detection (AquaSSM), F1 = 0.913 for microbial source attribution
(MicroBiomeNet), and AUROC = 0.992 for multimodal fusion---significantly
outperforming the best single modality ($p = 0.002$).
The behavioral channel provides minutes-scale initial alerts while genomic
channels enable diagnostic specificity.
\end{abstract}
